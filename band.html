Guns N’ Roses was formed in Los Angeles in 1985 by Rose and Stradlin. After changes in personnel, the band’s lineup stabilized with Rose as the vocalist, McKagan on bass, Adler on drums, and Slash and Stradlin on guitar. Signing with Geffen Records, they released the extended-play recording Live ?!*@ Like a Suicide in 1986, followed by the landmark album Appetite for Destruction in 1987. The music’s sizzling fury, with Rose’s wildcat howls matched by Slash’s guitar pyrotechnics, made the album a smash hit, with sales of more than 17 million.


Members

Axl Rose

Born and raised in Lafayette, Indiana, Rose moved in the early 1980s to Los Angeles, where he became active in the local hard rock scene and joined several bands, including Hollywood Rose and L.A. Guns. In 1985, he co-founded Guns N' Roses

Slash

Saul Hudson, mainly known as Slash, was born on July 23, 1965, and was raised in Stoke-on-Trent.In June 1985, Slash was asked by Axl Rose and Izzy Stradlin to join the newly founded Guns N' Roses

Duff McKagan

Before Guns N' Roses, was in a band called Road Crew with future Guns N' Roses bandmates Slash and Steven Adler. In 2000 he attended Seattle University, where he co-founded Velvet Revolver with ex Guns N' Roses band mates Slash and Matt Sorum he completed his accounting degree over the Internet.

Dizzy Reed

Dizzy Reed was born in Hinsdale, Illinois, USA. Along with Axl Rose, Dizzy is currently the only old-school member of Guns N' Roses still with the group. Officially joined Guns N' Roses in April 1990; however he did play a few shows with them in 1986, the year of their inception.

Richard Fortus

Aside from lead singer Axl Rose and keyboardist Dizzy Reed, Fortus is the longest-tenured member of Guns N' Roses, having been with the band continuously since 2002. Alongside his work in Guns N' Roses, Fortus is a former member of Love Spit Love, Thin Lizzy, and The Dead Daisies.

Frank Ferrer

Ferrer saw KISS in concert at Madison Square Garden and fell in love with rock music.In July 2006, Frank Ferrer joined Guns N' Roses during their European Tour. Initially expected to be just a touring drummer filling in for 2 weeks, Ferrer became the official drummer in late 2006.

Melissa Reese

Reese began singing and playing piano at age 4. Reese joined Guns N' Roses in March 2016 as the band's second keyboardist. Upon joining the band, she had two weeks to learn and master 50 songs to prepare for the tour, working 15 hours a day to learn them.

Timeline

  • Slash and Steven Adler Join Hollywood Rose

    Hollywood Rose was an early band in the Guns N' Roses story. The group was formed in 1983 by guitarist Chris Weber, Izzy Stradlin and Axl Rose. Along with several revolving bass players and drummer Johnny Kreis, the band recorded a few demos together and played some smaller shows. Internal problems led to Road Crew's Slash and Steven Adler coming in on the guitar and drums, respectively. Though Stradlin left for a bit, the introduction of Rose and Slash ultimately determined what would become Guns N' Roses' future.

    1984

  • Guns N' Roses Form

    Hollywood Rose eventually dissolved, and Los Angeles guitarist Tracii Guns was looking for a vocalist for his band L.A. Guns. Axl Rose and Izzy Stradlin joined him in 1985, and they went on to combine their names to become Guns N' Roses. Duff McKagan, who had recently played in Road Crew, was hired on bass.They called up Slash and Adler, whom they'd previously played with in Hollywood Rose and Road Crew, and the official lineup was finally complete. "The moment that we fuckin' slammed into our first chord, there was something. And we all knew it," McKagan later recalled, adding that Rose was the final element to click the second he started singing.

    1985

  • Guns N' Roses Sign to Geffen Records

    Tom Zutaut, was working for Geffen Records at the time and had been invited to one of GN'R's February shows at the Troubadour in 1986. After hearing a few songs in their set, he went back outside and advised the other label reps that the band wasn't good or worth taking on. On March 26, 1986, Guns N' Roses signed a "memo deal" with Geffen Records and received an advance of $75,000.

    1986

  • Welcome to the Breakthrough

    Despite what we know today about the album, Appetite for Destruction didn't do all that well in its first year."Welcome to the Jungle" was released as the first single that October, and David Geffen had to personally call MTV and ask them to play the accompanying music video.

    1988

  • An Unforgettable Night in St. Louis

    The infamous Riverport Riot was already brewing before the show even started.During “Rocket Queen,” the singer ordered guards to confiscate a camera from a member of the audience. When they didn’t comply, he took matters into his own hands — diving face-first off the stage and into the crowd. Once he climbed back onto the stage, he signaled the rest of the band to stop playing and announced he would be going home, smashing his microphone on the floor as he walked off. Confused fans started rioting, and Rose was later arrested for the occurrence.

    1991

  • The Spaghetti Incident?

    Guns N’ Roses released a set of punk cover songs titled The Spaghetti Incident. They would never tour behind it, and that lineup of the band would never release another studio album together again.

    1993

  • 'Not in This Lifetime' Tour

    Guns N' Roses announced their Not in This Lifetime Tour in 2016 — which would see Slash and Duff McKagan join Axl Rose on stage for the first time in 23 years. The reunion lasted into the end of 2019 and became the third highest-grossing tour in music history.
